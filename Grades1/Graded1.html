The University of Arkansas at Little Rock

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UALR) is a public research University in Little Rock, Arkansas.

Originally established as a junior college in 1927, it eventually evolved into a private four-year university named Little Rock University in 1957. Spanning 250 acres, UALR has over 56 building which, according to Wikipedia, include the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Sciences, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and the Ottenheimer Library. Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities that include a learning resource center, art galleries, KUAR public radio station, University Television, and a campus-wide wireless network.

Academics

The university has more than 100 undergraduate degrees and 60 graduate degrees, including graduate certificates, master's degrees, and doctorates, through both traditional and online courses. Students attend class in one of the university's three new colleges and a law school.
  1. College of Business, Health, and Human Services
  2. College of Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences, and Education
  3. Donaghey College of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics
  4. William H. Bowen School of Law
